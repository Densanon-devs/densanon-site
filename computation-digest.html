Vdura hikes its enterprise SSD pricing, now costs 22.6x more than hard drives

Tom's Hardware
  • Vdura has raised enterprise SSD prices sharply — now priced at 22.6x the per-terabyte cost of hard drives, reflecting a 472% increase — widening the cost gap between flash and spinning media at the high end.
  • The move runs counter to the long-term trend of flash cost parity with HDDs, and may push latency-tolerant HPC and storage workloads back toward tiered or hybrid disk architectures.
  • Enterprise storage buyers will need to reassess all-flash assumptions for large-capacity deployments where cost-per-TB is the primary constraint.
