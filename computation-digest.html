'CopprLink' destroys every eGPU standard in new test, achieves near-native-level performance with an RTX 5090 — setup requires $2,300 worth of additional hardware

Tom's Hardware
  • CopprLink is HighPoint's PCIe 5.0 x16 external GPU interconnect — it delivers the full bandwidth of a native motherboard slot over a copper cable, compared to Thunderbolt 5 eGPU setups limited to PCIe 4.0 x4 (~8 GB/s).
  • An RTX 5090 in the CopprLink enclosure matched the benchmark numbers of the same card plugged directly into the motherboard — the first external GPU setup to reach native-parity performance.
  • The barrier is cost: a $1,300 HighPoint enclosure plus a $1,000 PCIe 5.0 host adapter card are required, placing this firmly in enterprise and high-end workstation territory.
  • For rendering farms, ML inference nodes, or dense rack builds where hot-swapping GPUs without opening chassis has operational value, this bandwidth parity changes the viability calculus for external GPU architectures.

Why we spent 50+ hours retesting Intel's Core Ultra 270K Plus and 250K Plus

Tom's Hardware
  • The Core Ultra 270K Plus and 250K Plus are Arrow Lake refreshes whose initial review results were anomalously high — Tom's Hardware ran the full benchmark suite again from scratch to validate them.

FTRFS: New Fault-Tolerant File-System Proposed For Linux

Phoronix
  • FTRFS is a new Linux filesystem proposal focused on fault tolerance — its initial patch series hit the mailing list today for community review, positioning it as a more serious proposal than recent efforts like VMUFAT.
  • The fault-tolerant design angle targets use cases where data integrity under failure conditions is paramount (embedded systems, storage appliances, safety-critical compute), territory where ext4 and Btrfs are engineered for performance and flexibility rather than strict fault isolation.
  • As a brand-new proposal still in RFC stage, FTRFS will need extensive review before any merge window consideration — but its framing as a purpose-built fault-tolerant FS is a notable gap it is trying to fill in the Linux storage stack.

Btrfs Brings Performance Improvements, Shutdown ioctl Stable With Linux 7.1

Phoronix
  • Btrfs in Linux 7.1 receives another round of performance optimizations continuing the multi-cycle effort to close the throughput gap with ext4 on common workloads.
  • The shutdown ioctl — which allows userspace to cleanly stop a Btrfs filesystem without unmounting — graduates from experimental to stable, making it safe to use in production tooling and container orchestration environments.

NZXT to cough up $3.45 million over 'predatory' Flex PC rental scheme in RICO class-action settlement — in-debt customers to get up to $5,000 of relief, eligible renters to be granted ownership

Tom's Hardware
  • NZXT's Flex program was a PC rental-to-own scheme that drew a RICO (racketeering) class-action — not just a consumer protection claim — after allegedly using deceptive financing terms, hidden fees, and marketing tactics targeting minors.
  • The $3.45M settlement provides up to $5,000 in relief per customer who ended up in debt under the scheme, and converts eligible active rentals to outright ownership of the hardware.
  • RICO certification requires courts to find a plausible pattern of organized deceptive conduct — getting this far signals the allegations were substantiated, and sets a precedent for how predatory hardware financing schemes can be prosecuted.
