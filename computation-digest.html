Hardware & Chips

Scientists create electronic devices that function reliably at extreme temperatures

Tom's Hardware
  • Researchers demonstrated silicon-doped beta-gallium oxide transistors that operate across a 771°C range — from -271°C (near absolute zero) to 500°C — a span no silicon or GaN device can cover, opening doors for space, aerospace, and downhole industrial sensing without thermal management hardware.
  • Beta-gallium oxide's ultra-wide bandgap (~4.8 eV) is what enables this extreme range; the silicon doping technique used here is a practical manufacturing path, not just a lab curiosity, suggesting near-term transition from prototype to process node.

AMD's Lemonade SDK 10.3 Now 10x Smaller By Getting Rid Of Electron

Phoronix
  • AMD's open-source Lemonade local AI server dropped Electron entirely in v10.3, shrinking the install from ~300 MB to ~30 MB — a meaningful win for the on-device AI stack that targets AMD CPUs, GPUs, and NPUs without cloud dependency.
  • Removing Electron also improves startup latency and memory footprint at runtime, not just install size; for developers building local inference pipelines on AMD hardware, this is a more deployable base that doesn't carry a full Chromium runtime.

Phoronix
