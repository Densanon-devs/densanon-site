Math Takes Two: A Test for Emergent Mathematical Reasoning in Communication

ArXiv cs.AI
  • The paper introduces a two-agent math task where neither agent can solve the problem alone — cooperation and communication are structurally required, making it a clean probe for emergent reasoning.
  • Results show that even strong models struggle when mathematical reasoning must be distributed across agents, pointing to a meaningful gap between single-agent and multi-agent problem solving.

When Cow Urine Cures Constipation on YouTube: Limits of LLMs in Detecting Culture-specific Health Misinformation

ArXiv cs.CL
  • LLMs trained predominantly on English Western data miss a significant share of health misinformation rooted in non-Western cultural and traditional medicine contexts — the models often can't recognize the claim as false because they lack cultural grounding.
  • The finding has real stakes: YouTube health content in Hindi, Bengali, and similar languages reaches hundreds of millions of users, and generic LLM safety filters are not calibrated for it.

Shared Lexical Task Representations Explain Behavioral Variability in LLMs

ArXiv cs.CL
  • The study finds that LLMs form shared internal representations for lexically similar tasks, which explains why performance varies predictably across surface-form prompt rewrites.
  • This has direct implications for prompt engineering: task similarity at the token level — not just semantic similarity — shapes how reliably a model generalizes.
