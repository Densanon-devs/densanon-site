Research & Papers

PExA: Parallel Exploration Agent for Complex Text-to-SQL

ArXiv cs.AI
  • Reframes text-to-SQL as a software test-coverage problem — run parallel candidate queries and score by coverage rather than picking the single best guess
  • Targets the latency-performance tradeoff that plagues sequential chain-of-thought agents on complex SQL schemas
  • Parallel exploration branches from multiple schema decompositions simultaneously, then merges the highest-coverage result
  • Approach generalizes to other structured-output tasks where ground-truth can be approximated by test execution

The Power of Power Law: Asymmetry Enables Compositional Reasoning

ArXiv cs.AI
  • Challenges the intuition that reweighting training data toward a uniform distribution improves reasoning — power-law skew is actually load-bearing
  • Low-frequency knowledge follows a long tail; flattening the distribution strips compositional signals needed for rare concept combinations
  • Provides a theoretical account of why curated 'balanced' datasets can hurt compositional generalization at test time
  • Implications for dataset curation: preserve natural frequency ratios rather than aggressively upsampling rare categories

Large Language Models Explore by Latent Distilling

ArXiv cs.CL
  • Standard stochastic sampling yields surface-level lexical variation but not genuine semantic diversity — a real ceiling for test-time scaling via best-of-N
  • Proposes Exploratory Sampling: distills diverse latent directions from the model's own representation space to guide generation toward semantically distinct outputs
  • Semantic exploration improves best-of-N and majority-vote aggregation without requiring larger models or more compute per token
  • Mechanism is self-contained — no ensemble, external verifier, or separate reward model required

GAIA-v2-LILT: Multilingual Adaptation of Agent Benchmark beyond Translation

ArXiv cs.CL
  • Machine-translated versions of GAIA break benchmark validity — tool names, URLs, and procedural steps don't survive MT faithfully for agentic tasks
  • LILT (Localization-Informed Linguistic Transformation) applies culture-aware localization rather than string-level translation
  • First multilingual GAIA variant requiring non-English web navigation and reasoning, not just non-English question text
  • Exposes a systematic gap: agents with strong English GAIA scores fail to generalize even to closely related European languages

ADE: Adaptive Dictionary Embeddings — Scaling Multi-Anchor Representations to Large Language Models

ArXiv cs.CL
  • Traditional single-vector word embeddings create representational bottlenecks for polysemous words — ADE assigns each word multiple anchor vectors weighted by context
  • Scales multi-anchor representations to LLM scale without proportional parameter increase by using a learned dictionary of shared anchors
  • Demonstrates improved performance on tasks where word sense disambiguation matters: metaphor, legal text, technical jargon
  • Designed as a drop-in replacement for standard embedding layers with modest training overhead

Talkie: a 13B LLM trained only on pre-1931 text used Claude Sonnet to help test the model and judge its output

r/ClaudeAI
  • Alec Radford (author of GPT, CLIP, and Whisper), Nick Levine, and David Duvenaud trained a 13B model exclusively on text published before December 31, 1930 — no WWII, no internet, no post-1930 concepts
  • Trained on 260B tokens of pre-1931 English text; knowledge cutoff is enforced by corpus selection rather than instruction
  • Claude Sonnet was used as the automated judge to evaluate historical accuracy and temporal consistency of Talkie's outputs
  • Demonstrates using a frontier model as an eval harness for a specialized domain-bounded model — clean separation of generation and judgment

Qwen Introduced FlashQLA

r/LocalLLaMA
  • FlashQLA delivers 2-3x forward speedup and 2x backward speedup for linear attention via high-performance kernels built on TileLang
  • Uses gate-driven automatic intra-card communication parallelism to keep compute dense across attention layers
  • Hardware-friendly algebraic reformulation avoids the bottlenecks that make naive linear attention slower than standard attention on modern GPUs
  • Targeted explicitly at agentic AI on personal devices — relevant for anyone running Qwen models locally at inference time
