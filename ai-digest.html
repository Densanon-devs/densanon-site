Research & Papers

Hallucination as output-boundary misclassification: a composite abstention architecture for language models

ArXiv cs.CL
  • The paper reframes hallucination as a misclassification at the model's output boundary — the moment it emits internally generated text as if it were grounded evidence.
  • A composite abstention architecture is proposed that intercepts high-uncertainty completions before emission rather than correcting them post-hoc.
  • The approach shows measurable reductions in unsupported claim rate without proportional drops in task completion.

ArXiv cs.AI
