Xpertbench: Expert Level Tasks with Rubrics-Based Evaluation

ArXiv cs.AI
  • Standard benchmarks plateau too easily — Xpertbench uses rubric-graded expert tasks to expose real capability gaps that leaderboards hide.
  • Rubric-based scoring catches partial credit and reasoning quality, not just final-answer accuracy, giving a more honest capability picture.
  • Results suggest frontier models still struggle with genuinely expert-level tasks, tempering hype about near-human professional performance.

Single-Agent LLMs Outperform Multi-Agent Systems on Multi-Hop Reasoning

ArXiv cs.CL
  • When compute is held constant, a single capable LLM beats multi-agent pipelines on complex multi-hop reasoning tasks — the overhead eats the gains.
  • Coordination costs and error propagation across agents explain much of the gap, especially when subtasks are tightly interdependent.
  • A useful corrective for teams adding agent layers by default — the simpler architecture often wins when you normalize for resources.
