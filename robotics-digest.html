Research & Papers

AI-evolved "legged metamachines" that self-repair and refuse to die

Northwestern / PNAS
  • Northwestern engineers created modular robots where each unit has its own motor, battery, and computer — AI evolved novel body configurations no human would design
  • Metamachines undulate like seals, bound like lizards, or spring like kangaroos across gravel, grass, mud, and uneven terrain without retraining
  • When a leg breaks off, the remaining modules adapt and keep moving — the first robots to set foot outdoors after evolving entirely inside a computer
