Sony AI table tennis robot outplays elite human players

Robohub
  • Sony AI robot Ace is the first robot to defeat elite human players in a competitive physical sport, published in Nature.
  • The December 2025 match saw Ace beat Yamato Kawamata, demonstrating real-time perception, paddle rotation, and dynamic strategy under adversarial conditions.
  • Separates AI physical-sport performance from purely digital victories like chess or Go -- a genuine closed-loop robotics milestone.
  • Sets a new benchmark for real-time robotic control under full human adversarial pressure.

HALO: Hybrid Auto-encoded Locomotion with Learned Latent Dynamics, Poincar\'e Maps, and Regions of Attraction

ArXiv cs.RO
  • Introduces HALO, a reduced-order model framework for legged robots that learns latent dynamics for hybrid contact-switching systems.
  • Uses Poincare maps and regions of attraction to provide formal stability guarantees, filling a gap that classical template models leave open.
  • Targets high-dimensional humanoid and quadruped locomotion where hand-designed models fail to generalize.
  • Could improve sim-to-real transfer by grounding controllers in provably stable manifolds.

Gated Memory Policy

ArXiv cs.RO
  • Proposes a policy architecture that adaptively gates memory based on whether a manipulation task is Markovian or non-Markovian.
  • Key finding: naively extending memory to Markovian tasks hurts performance -- selective gating activates history only when needed.
  • Addresses a practical pain point in robot learning where a single policy type rarely fits all task structures.
  • Results span single-trial and multi-trial interaction settings, suggesting broad applicability across manipulation pipelines.

Proposed Chinese Robot Ban Is Latest U.S. Tech Sovereignty Move

IEEE Spectrum Robotics
  • The American Security Robotics Act, bipartisan and sponsored by Senators Cotton and Schumer and Rep. Stefanik, would bar U.S. government procurement of Chinese ground robots.
  • Covered categories include humanoids, quadruped dogs, and crawlers -- broad enough to affect most major Chinese robotics OEMs.
  • Positions robotics explicitly alongside semiconductors and telecom in the U.S. tech sovereignty legislative agenda.
  • Could accelerate domestic and allied-nation robot supply chains if enacted, creating competitive tailwinds for U.S. and European vendors.
