The Strategic Value of Legacy Components in Automation

Robotics & Automation News
  • Most factory floors are hybrid environments -- cutting-edge IIoT layers sitting on top of PLCs and actuators that are 10-20 years old, and the legacy hardware isn't going away anytime soon.
  • Ripping and replacing legacy components is rarely cost-justified; the strategic play is extending component life through predictive maintenance, adapters, and software shims.
  • Robot integrators that co-exist with legacy equipment win more deployments than systems demanding greenfield installs.

Circular economy in electronics: Extending hardware life

Robotics & Automation News
  • Global e-waste runs in the tens of millions of tonnes annually while semiconductor shortages simultaneously leave factories starved for parts -- a paradox that circular approaches directly address.
  • Refurbishing, remanufacturing, and component harvesting from end-of-life hardware can reduce procurement costs and supply-chain exposure for automation shops.
  • Robot OEMs that design for disassembly and part reuse gain a sustainability story that increasingly matters in enterprise procurement decisions.
