Research & Papers

asRoBallet: Closing the Sim2Real Gap via Friction-Aware Reinforcement Learning for Underactuated Spherical Dynamics

ArXiv cs.RO
  • First reported RL deployment on a humanoid ballbot -- a canonical underactuated, nonholonomic platform that has historically resisted learned controllers due to instability under surface variation.
  • Introduces friction-aware domain randomization specifically targeting the rolling contact between ball and floor, which is the dominant sim-to-real failure mode for this platform.
  • Validates that RL can transfer to physical ballbot hardware when the right physical parameters are randomized during training.

Libra-VLA: Achieving Learning Equilibrium via Asynchronous Coarse-to-Fine Dual-System

ArXiv cs.RO
  • Replaces the monolithic generation paradigm in Vision-Language-Action models with an asynchronous dual-system: a coarse semantic planner running at low frequency and a fine-grained action executor at full control rate.
  • Directly addresses the latency vs. quality trade-off that makes current VLA models impractical for real-time manipulation -- the high-level system does not block the low-level executor.
  • Targets generalist robotic manipulation where grounding language instructions into physical actions remains brittle in single-network approaches.

VISION-SLS: Safe Perception-Based Control from Learned Visual Representations via System Level Synthesis

ArXiv cs.RO
  • Provides provable constraint satisfaction guarantees for nonlinear output-feedback controllers operating directly from high-resolution RGB images -- a hard safety result rarely achieved for vision-based control.
  • Handles partial observability, sensor noise, and nonlinear dynamics simultaneously through System Level Synthesis with calibrated uncertainty bounds.
  • Practical for deployment scenarios where worst-case constraint violation (e.g., collision) is unacceptable, not just average performance.

MotionBricks: Scalable Real-Time Motions with Modular Latent Generative Model and Smart Primitives

ArXiv cs.RO
  • Identifies the production gap: research-grade generative motion synthesis is not real-time interactive -- practical deployment still relies on handcrafted state machines and transition graphs.
  • Proposes modular 'motion bricks' -- composable latent primitives that can be assembled and blended at runtime without rerunning a full generative model.
  • Targets game engines, simulation, and character animation pipelines where a 30-60fps hard latency budget rules out current diffusion or autoregressive motion models.

An analysis of sensor selection for fruit picking with suction-based grippers

ArXiv cs.RO
  • Systematically evaluates force, vacuum pressure, and vision sensor modalities for detecting successful picks on compliant fruit with suction grippers.
  • Targets the specific failure modes of variable stem attachment and occlusion that cause silent pick failures -- the robot doesn't know it missed, increasing crop damage.
  • Provides a sensor selection framework with practical guidance for agricultural robotics teams choosing instrumentation for suction-based end effectors.

Starship passes 10 million deliveries as autonomous delivery moves toward mainstream adoption

Robotics & Automation News
  • Starship's sidewalk robot fleet has crossed 10 million cumulative deliveries -- a concrete operational scale milestone that separates it from pilot-stage autonomous delivery programs.
  • Demonstrates that AMR delivery is commercially viable today on geofenced, well-mapped urban routes even as fully open-world autonomy remains unsolved.
  • The milestone reframes sidewalk robotics: the question is no longer whether the technology works in controlled conditions but how fast geofenced coverage can expand.

GFT Technologies AI-powered robots move from inspection to action for automakers

The Robot Report
  • GFT has closed the loop from computer vision defect detection to physical defect removal on automotive factory floors, using Google Cloud AI to drive actuation decisions -- not just flag issues for humans.
  • This is a meaningful step beyond passive inspection robots: the system takes autonomous corrective action, reducing dependence on human operators for remediation.
  • Deployed in automotive manufacturing where part rejection costs are high and defect-removal speed directly affects line throughput.

The Robot Report

Why self-driving cars took longer than expected to reach real-world deployment

r/robotics
  • CMU's Martial Hebert distinguishes technology readiness from deployment readiness -- the core sensing and ML capabilities were sufficient years earlier, but operational complexity in uncontrolled environments was consistently underestimated.
  • Heavily mapped, geofenced corridors (Waymo's operational domain) are fundamentally different engineering problems from open-world urban driving -- a gap that has still not closed.
  • Useful calibration for any robotics team estimating deployment timelines: environmental variability and edge-case density are usually the binding constraint, not algorithm performance on benchmarks.
