Research & Papers

RecoverFormer: End-to-End Contact-Aware Recovery for Humanoid Robots

ArXiv cs.RO
  • RecoverFormer learns fall-recovery policies that explicitly model contact forces during ground interaction, producing recovery motions that adapt to the robot's actual posture rather than replaying a fixed get-up sequence.
  • End-to-end contact awareness closes a persistent gap in humanoid control: getting back up reliably after unexpected disturbances is a prerequisite for any real-world deployment, and prior controllers handle it poorly.

SwarmDrive: Semantic V2V Coordination for Latency-Constrained Cooperative Autonomous Driving

ArXiv cs.RO
  • SwarmDrive routes semantic scene summaries between nearby vehicles instead of raw sensor streams, slashing the bandwidth required for cooperative perception while keeping cloud round-trips out of the critical path.
  • Reducing cloud dependency for multi-vehicle coordination is a key step toward practical deployment -- latency spikes and connectivity gaps are the failure mode that makes cloud-dependent AV cooperation unsafe in real traffic.

Gradient-based planning for world models at longer horizons

Robohub
  • The GRASP planner lifts trajectory optimization into virtual states so gradient updates run in parallel across the full horizon rather than sequentially, making long-horizon planning over learned dynamics practical for the first time at scale.
  • Adding stochasticity directly to state iterates and reshaping the loss landscape addresses the local-minima problem that has hampered gradient-based planning through world models -- a meaningful methodological step for model-based RL.
