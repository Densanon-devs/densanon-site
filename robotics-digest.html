Research & Papers

LEGO: Latent-space Exploration for Geometry-aware Optimization of Humanoid Kinematic Design

ArXiv cs.RO
  • Accepted at ICRA 2026; addresses the lack of principled, automated methods for humanoid upper-body kinematic design — traditionally done by gut feel and manual iteration.
  • Learns a compact latent space from existing mechanical designs using screw-theory joint-axis representations and isometric manifold learning, making gradient-free design-space search tractable.
  • Loss function is derived directly from human motion data via retargeting and Procrustes analysis — no hand-crafted task objective needed, just existing motion capture data.
  • Demonstrates automated discovery of novel robot morphologies that could not be found through intuition-driven design, a concrete step toward hardware/motion co-optimization.

Generative Simulation for Policy Learning in Physical Human-Robot Interaction

ArXiv cs.RO
  • First text2sim2real pipeline for physical human-robot interaction: a natural language description generates a complete simulation environment (soft-body human model, scene layout, robot trajectories) with no manual setup.
  • LLMs and VLMs handle environment synthesis; policies trained via imitation learning on segmented point clouds from the synthetic data.
  • Zero-shot sim-to-real transfer validated on two assistive tasks — scratch assistance and bathing — achieving over 80% success rate in user studies despite unscripted human motion.
  • Directly attacks the data scarcity bottleneck in pHRI; the generative pipeline can produce large-scale diverse training datasets on demand rather than requiring costly physical demonstrations.

Toward Hardware-Agnostic Quadrupedal World Models via Morphology Conditioning

ArXiv cs.RO
  • First world model for quadrupedal locomotion that generalizes zero-shot to new robot morphologies — validated across Boston Dynamics Spot and Unitree Go1, which have meaningfully different kinematics.
  • Key design choice: explicitly condition the generative dynamics model on robot engineering specs via a physical morphology encoder, rather than implicit system identification which creates adaptation lag.
  • Rewards are normalized across embodiments, so the same trained model plans and behaves for robots it was not directly trained on without any retraining step.
  • Authors are candid that the model is a distribution-bounded interpolator within the quadrupedal family, not a universal physics engine — useful scope clarity for practitioners.

Task-Aware Bimanual Affordance Prediction via VLM-Guided Semantic-Geometric Reasoning

ArXiv cs.RO
  • Solves the joint problem of contact-region localization and arm assignment in bimanual manipulation — geometry-only planners fail at arm allocation because it requires semantic task understanding.
  • Pipeline fuses multi-view RGB-D into a unified 3D scene, generates global 6-DoF grasp candidates, then uses VLM queries to filter by task-relevant affordance regions and assign left vs. right arm.
  • Evaluated on a dual-arm platform across nine real-world tasks in four categories (parallel manipulation, coordinated stabilization, tool use, human handover); consistently outperforms both geometric and semantic baselines.
  • Generalizes across object categories without category-specific training — VLM grounding provides the cross-category transfer capability that prior task-specific models could not.

Product & Industry

Unitree's H1 robot hits 10m/s sprint speed, getting close to 100M world record

Hacker News Robotics
  • Unitree H1 (~62 kg, 80 cm combined leg length) achieved ~10 m/s — within 4% of Usain Bolt's 100m average pace of 10.44 m/s — on a human-proportioned bipedal platform.
  • CEO Wang Xingxing publicly predicts breaking the 10-second 100m barrier by mid-2026, which would mean surpassing Bolt's 9.58s world record on equivalent average speed.
  • Observers note the achievement demands tight integration across perception, actuation, and learned control policies — the control stack maturity is the meaningful signal, not just raw speed.
  • Multiple Chinese humanoid manufacturers (Unitree, Tien Kung Ultra, MirrorMe) are actively competing in robot sprinting, suggesting dynamic bipedal locomotion is maturing from lab curiosity to competitive benchmark.

Chinese automaker Chery begins selling humanoid robot to consumers for $42,000 each

Robotics & Automation News

Taiwan launches national robotics center with $629 million startup funding plan

Robotics & Automation News
  • NT$20 billion ($629M) committed across 2026–2029, on top of an earlier NT$10B ($331M) subsidy program — total public commitment now exceeds $960M over the period.
  • The National Center for AI Robotics (NCAIR) operates under the National Institutes of Applied Research and is tasked with development, testing, and talent training; inaugurated by President Lai as part of the Ten AI Initiatives.
  • Explicit target: at least three new robotics startups by 2029, with focus on home care, high-risk industrial automation, and healthcare/hospitality — moving Taiwan beyond component supply into complete system integration.
  • Strategic driver is demographic: Taiwan's aging population and workforce decline make domestic automation a national-security-level priority, not just an economic play.

Tools & Practical

ROS 2: The next generation for robust and scalable robotics applications

Robotics & Automation News
  • Replaces ROS 1's centralized master node with DDS (Data Distribution Service) middleware — eliminating the single point of failure and enabling decentralized multi-robot deployments natively.
  • Real-time support with per-topic Quality of Service (QoS) controls lets engineers tune reliability vs. latency independently per data stream — critical for safety-critical actuation loops.
  • Built-in security layer (encryption, authentication, access control) addresses a fundamental gap in ROS 1 that was increasingly untenable as robots enter networked factory and medical environments.
  • Cross-platform support now covers Linux, Windows, and embedded targets — broadening deployment beyond research-lab Ubuntu setups to production-grade environments.

The "Hand Farms" of India: Fueling the AI Robot Revolution with Human Motion

Hacker News Robotics
  • Data capture facilities in Bengaluru employ workers wearing head-mounted cameras to record first-person manipulation videos (towel folding, box stacking, utensil sorting) — the physical training data layer that robot foundation models depend on.
  • Objectways alone employs 2,000+ people producing hundreds of such videos daily; buyers include Scale AI (Meta-backed), Figure AI, and Micro1.
  • Workers earn $120–250/month for generating the embodied AI training data underpinning multi-billion-dollar robotics companies — the labor economics of the data pipeline are stark.
  • Highlights an under-discussed supply chain reality: the 'data problem' in robotics is not only a sim-to-real transfer challenge, it is also a global human labor infrastructure operating largely out of public view.
