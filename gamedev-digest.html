Stormgate rushing offline mode after losing server access to an AI company

Game Developer
  • Frost Giant Studios lost access to server infrastructure it had been relying on through a third-party AI company, forcing an emergency sprint to ship an offline mode for Stormgate.
  • The situation is a real-world case study in the fragility of depending on external-vendor infrastructure during Early Access — particularly from AI-sector companies with volatile business models.
  • The silver lining: adding offline mode under duress is a commitment to player preservation that many live-service studios only make under pressure, so the outcome benefits the community even if the circumstances are chaotic.

The Last of Us Online was around '80%' complete when Naughty Dog canceled it

Game Developer
  • New reporting puts The Last of Us Online at approximately 80% completion at the time of cancellation — far further along than previously understood, making the decision to kill it even more striking.

A Game Pass for indie games: meet Indie Pass, a $6.99-a-month subscription launching with 70 titles

GamesIndustry.biz
  • Indie Pass is launching at $6.99/month with 70 titles, positioning itself explicitly as a Game Pass-style subscription service focused entirely on indie games.
  • The economics for participating developers will be the critical question — subscription revenue splits in catalog services can be brutal for smaller titles, and the itch.io-vs-Steam discovery dynamics will determine whether the 70-title launch catalog is a strength or a liability.
