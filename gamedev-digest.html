Industry & News

French consumer group sues Ubisoft over shutdown of The Crew

Game Developer
  • A French consumer advocacy group has filed suit against Ubisoft over the total shutdown of The Crew, citing laws protecting consumers' right to continue using purchased software.
  • The case is directly tied to the Stop Killing Games campaign, which has collected over 1.3 million signatures — a level of public pressure that now has legal backing in at least one EU jurisdiction.
  • This is potentially the most consequential legal challenge yet to publishers' practice of fully deactivating paid games via server shutdown, with implications for the entire live-service industry.

Call of Duty: Black Ops 7 appears to earn an estimated $30,000 on Steam per negative review

Game Developer
  • Analysis suggests Black Ops 7 is generating roughly $30,000 in Steam revenue for every negative review it receives — a striking illustration of how review-bombing correlates inversely with sales velocity while the absolute numbers still skew enormous.
  • The data point reframes how to read review scores for mega-franchises: a 'Mixed' rating on a Call of Duty title means something very different commercially than the same rating on an indie game.
  • For smaller studios watching this, the takeaway is that brand inertia absorbs review punishment in ways that simply don't apply outside the top tier of established IPs.

Goat Simulator maker Coffee Stain shutters Malmo studio

Game Developer

  • The closure continues a pattern of mid-tier publishers consolidating satellite offices even when their flagship titles are performing well.

'More time innovating; less time writing code:' Arc Raiders publisher Nexon believes AI will liberate its workforce

Game Developer
  • Nexon has publicly framed AI adoption as workforce liberation rather than reduction, positioning automation as a creativity multiplier — a narrative that is becoming standard corporate language around AI-driven headcount decisions.
  • The statement comes alongside reports of project cancellations, which complicates the 'liberation' framing and raises questions about whether AI is expanding capacity or justifying cuts.
  • Worth tracking as a bellwether: Nexon is one of the larger publishers to put this stance on the record, and how their workforce evolves over the next 12-18 months will either validate or undermine the argument.

US Patent Office rejects Nintendo's 'summon subcharacter and let it fight' patent

GamesIndustry.biz
  • The US Patent Office has rejected Nintendo's attempt to patent a mechanic broadly covering summoning a companion character to fight on the player's behalf — a move that would have had wide chilling effects on the genre.
  • The rejection signals that the USPTO is applying meaningful prior art scrutiny to broad gameplay mechanic patents, which is a modest but meaningful win for the indie developer community.
  • Nintendo's aggressive patent strategy in recent years has been a source of concern; this outcome doesn't stop the behavior but does show the system can push back.

PlayCanvas Can Now Load Gracia's 4D Gaussian Splats

80 Level
  • PlayCanvas has added support for Gracia's 4D Gaussian Splats — essentially volumetric video captured with Gaussian splatting that includes a time dimension, enabling playback of real-world motion in browser-based 3D.
  • This is a technically significant milestone: photorealistic volumetric video in a browser without plugins, which opens doors for web-based virtual production previsualization, interactive experiences, and marketing content.
  • Gaussian splatting continues to move fast as a production-ready format; its arrival in a WebGL engine is a strong signal that the toolchain is maturing beyond research demos.
