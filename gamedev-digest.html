GameMaker incorporates Claude Code to enable AI-assisted workflows

Game Developer
  • GameMaker is integrating Claude Code directly into the engine as an optional AI-assisted workflow layer — opt-in, not forced on all users.
  • Head Russell Kay framed the goal as giving users 'who might benefit from AI the option to do so,' signaling a measured rollout rather than an all-in AI pivot.
  • This makes GameMaker one of the first established indie engines to embed a production LLM coding tool, putting it ahead of Godot and Unity on this specific integration.
  • For solo devs and non-programmers, GML scripting assistance inside the editor could meaningfully lower the barrier to entry in an engine already known for accessibility.

'The company as a whole no longer exists:' GreedFall developer Spiders confirms closure

Game Developer
  • Spiders, the French AA studio behind GreedFall, has confirmed full closure after publisher Nacon filed for insolvency earlier in 2026 following a failed bond loan repayment.
  • This is a cascade failure: Nacon's financial collapse is pulling down multiple studios in its portfolio — not a single project underperforming.
  • Spiders occupied a specific niche (narrative-heavy RPGs on modest budgets); its loss removes one of the few studios working in that exact space.
  • Underscores the structural fragility of AA studios dependent on publishers with leveraged balance sheets — a risk that has now materialized visibly.

Xbox hardware revenue drops 33% year-on-year during Q3

GamesIndustry.biz
  • Xbox hardware revenue fell 33% year-on-year in Q3 2026, with total gaming revenue down $380 million — while monthly active users hit an all-time record.
  • The split confirms Microsoft's deliberate trajectory: hardware as a loss leader (or exit), with real business value in Game Pass subscriptions and multi-platform content.
  • Xbox content and services also dipped 5%, meaning even the subscription layer isn't fully absorbing the hardware decline yet.

Sony confirms DRM policy on PS4 and PS5 is a 'one-time check'

GamesIndustry.biz
  • Sony officially clarified that the DRM check for newly purchased digital PS4/PS5 games is required only once after purchase, not on every session or boot.
  • The clarification comes amid sustained consumer pressure around digital game ownership and preservation — Sony had to state the policy explicitly rather than leave it ambiguous.
  • Once the single check completes, offline play is unrestricted: better than some feared, but still tethers ownership to an initial internet connection.
  • Relevant for devs shipping on PS: how the platform handles DRM directly affects how you communicate offline functionality in store listings and player support.

Techniques & Learning

The secret to Overwatch's revitalization? Over-communicating.

Game Developer
  • Overwatch game director Aaron Keller presented at GDC 2026 that the core turnaround was continuous, proactive player communication — not just feature updates.
  • 'Our communication couldn't just be reactive or occasional. It really had to be continuous,' Keller said, describing a deliberate culture shift in how the team engaged the community.
  • The lesson generalizes beyond live service: transparency about decisions, roadmap changes, and failures builds trust that reactive damage-control cannot recover.
  • Practical for indie devs: a consistent devlog cadence and honest patch notes carry more weight than polished but infrequent announcements.

Inkle's Jon Ingold on why self-publishing is where it's at

GamesIndustry.biz
  • Inkle co-founder Jon Ingold laid out the financial and creative case for self-publishing at the London Games Festival's Self-Publishing Toolkit event in April 2026.
  • Inkle (80 Days, Pendragon, Overboard!) has sustained a profitable studio without a publisher across multiple titles — Ingold's argument is grounded in actual P&L, not ideology.
  • Core thesis: keeping 100% of revenue and maintaining creative control outweighs a publisher's marketing firepower, especially for narrative-heavy games with strong word-of-mouth potential.
  • A data point worth reading alongside the Spiders/Nacon story: publisher dependency is the variable that collapsed that studio.
