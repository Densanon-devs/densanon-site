Xbox will reevaluate exclusivity and target daily active players under new leadership

Game Developer
  • Asha Sharma and Matt Booty's joint memo signals a shift from game count to engagement depth as Xbox's primary metric.

  • Daily active player counts replace revenue/sales as the internal north star, echoing mobile and streaming playbooks.

Why Xbox took Call of Duty out of Game Pass

GamesIndustry.biz
  • Call of Duty will now arrive on Game Pass roughly a year after launch instead of day-one, reversing a post-Activision acquisition promise.
  • Game Pass Ultimate drops from $29.99 to $22.99/month; PC Game Pass falls to $13.99 — a clear move to grow the subscriber base.
  • The shift suggests day-one AAA on Game Pass cannibalizes too much unit revenue for premium titles.
  • Indies and mid-tier titles remain the safer day-one candidates; blockbusters may follow a 'delay then subscribe' model.

GamesIndustry.biz
  • The 'Microsoft Gaming' umbrella is gone — the division is Xbox again, consolidating brand identity after years of diffusion.

Atari acquires emulation studio Implicit Conversions

Game Developer
  • The deal gives Atari ownership of Syrup, Implicit Conversions' proprietary emulation technology.
  • Syrup is notable for high-accuracy emulation of older hardware — an asset for Atari's classic catalog revival strategy.
  • Acquiring in-house emulation tech reduces reliance on open-source solutions and gives Atari tighter control over licensing and distribution.
  • Signals Atari is betting on nostalgia and preservation as a sustainable revenue line, not just one-off retro collections.

Tools to tell if your game is broken

  • Matthew Tighe (ported Cult of the Lamb, Art of Rally) launched Gameworks to productize a decade of console porting diagnostic knowledge.

German game industry employment drops 3%, marking second year of decline

GamesIndustry.biz
  • Germany's game sector shed jobs for the second consecutive year, down 3% — a sign that consolidation pressures are still working through European studios.
  • Government funding has provided a partial buffer, but international consolidation is the primary driver of headcount losses.
  • Recovery is described as slow and uneven; smaller independent studios are faring better than subsidiaries of global publishers.
  • A regional indicator that the broader global studio contraction trend hasn't bottomed out yet.
