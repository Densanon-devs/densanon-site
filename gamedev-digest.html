Breakdown: Sculpting a Dwarf King Using ZBrush and Substance 3D Painter

80 Level
  • ZBrush handled high-poly sculpting and tertiary narrative details (wrinkles, age marks); surface wear and scratches were moved to Substance 3D Painter for non-destructive flexibility.
  • Skin texturing used layered subdermis color variation (reds, greens, purples, yellows) before building hypodermis and subsurface scattering passes — a multi-layer approach that avoids overworking the mesh.
  • Velvet material was faked by adjusting normal map color channels to redirect light reflection, mimicking velvet's characteristic sheen without a dedicated shader.
  • Key workflow advice: build a selective reference board, design topology for animation-ready deformation from the start, and create engine-specific export presets rather than relying on defaults.

How to Experiment with Metals, Textures and Materials to Create a Sword

80 Level
  • ZBrush's Morph Target brush was used extensively to roll back sculpt changes without losing base form — a useful technique for preventing over-sculpting on hard-surface props.
  • Metal calibration (forged steel, iron, silver, bronze, gold) was done in a Spec/Gloss workflow for precise reflectivity control; Damascus steel was treated artistically rather than realistically for better visual appeal.
  • Retopology used a hybrid approach: ZBrush auto-retopo for broad areas, 3ds Max for small details, and 3D-Coat for manual blade work — choosing the right tool per region rather than committing to one pipeline.
  • Rizom UV for unwrapping, Marmoset Toolbag for baking and rendering, Marvelous Designer for grip wrap fabric simulation.

Jesse Miettinen Demonstrates Grunge Generator On Furniture Assets

80 Level
  • Free Blender add-on (available on Gumroad) that automates weathering effects — streaks, edge wear, ground contact, and moss — using a single shader driven by per-object parameters.
  • Originally built for architectural environments in a Last of Us-style pipeline; Miettinen shows it scales equally well to smaller furniture assets.

The Death of Character in Game Console Interfaces

Hacker News Game Dev
  • The author argues that seventh-gen console UIs (Xbox 360 Blades, PS3 XMB, Wii) had genuine personality through sound design, visual identity, and theming — all stripped away by flat minimalism starting with the eighth generation.
  • Current PS5 and Xbox Series interfaces are critiqued as functionally identical: grid layouts of rounded boxes that prioritize ad placement over player atmosphere.
  • Frames the decline as a combination of flat UI design trends and corporate pressure to surface storefronts, crowding out the environmental storytelling that older dashboard menus provided.

Haunt, the 70s text adventure game, is now playable on a website

Hacker News Game Dev
  • Haunt (1979) is a notoriously difficult text adventure whose puzzles were based on the developer's knowledge of Hanna-Barbera cartoons (Jonny Quest, The Herculoids) — obscure cultural references even at the time of release.
  • The web port features a faithful CRT terminal aesthetic: phosphor glow with selectable display modes (green, amber, white), scanlines, and perspective-transformed screen bezel.
  • The developer acknowledged the game 'violated most, if not all, of the design guidelines for good interactive fiction' — making it a useful historical case study in puzzle design anti-patterns.
